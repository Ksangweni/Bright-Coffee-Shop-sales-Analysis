# Bright-Coffee-Shop-sales-Analysis

**Overview**
This case study analyzes historical transactional data from Bright Coffee Shop to provide actionable business insights for a newly appointed CEO. The goal was to transform raw sales records into clear strategies for revenue growth, product optimization, and store performance improvement.
Approach
The dataset included attributes such as transaction dates, times, product categories, store locations, unit prices, quantities, and daily revenue. The analysis was structured into three classes:
- Revenue Analysis > Identified revenue drivers, spend brackets, and peak sales intervals.
- Product Analysis > Highlighted best‑selling items, underperforming categories, and premium product opportunities.
- Store Analysis > Compared performance across locations and time slots to uncover unique strengths.

**Insights**
- Coffee, tea, and drinking chocolate are the backbone of revenue.
- Large‑sized products consistently outperform regular sizes.
- Brunch is the most profitable time slot, while lunch underperforms.
- Each store has unique strengths: Astoria (late lunch), Hell’s Kitchen (brunch), Lower Manhattan (breakfast).
- Weekday sales dominate over weekends, suggesting untapped weekend potential.
- Premium and specialty products (e.g., Civet Cat beans, organic chocolate) show strong demand.

**Tools Used**
- SQL & Databricks > Data cleaning, transformations, and aggregations as well as for data storage and processing pipeline.
- Excel > Viewing tables, Pivot tables, dashboards, and visual storytelling.
- Miro → Planning and architecture diagrams.
- Canva > Gannt Chart for project planning
- PowerPoint > Final presentation of insights and recommendations.
